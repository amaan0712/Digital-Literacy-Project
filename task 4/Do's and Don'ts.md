###### Do's



* Verify information before sharing — cross-check news or claims across at least two credible sources. Sharing misinformation, even unintentionally, damages your credibility online.
* Understand how algorithms work — social media platforms show you content designed to keep you engaged, not inform you. Actively seek out diverse perspectives outside your feed bubble.
* Use strong, unique passwords for each platform — and enable two-factor authentication. One compromised account can cascade into others if you reuse passwords.
* Know what data you're giving away — read (or at least skim) what permissions an app requests. A flashlight app doesn't need access to your contacts.
* Build a positive digital footprint intentionally — post work you're proud of, engage in meaningful discussions, and let your online presence reflect the professional you're becoming.





###### Don'ts



* Don't click links in DMs from unknown accounts — even if the message looks urgent or official. Phishing attacks are increasingly disguised as platform notifications or prize alerts.
* Don't assume private messages stay private — anything you type on a platform is stored on their servers. "Private" is a setting, not a guarantee.
* Don't use public Wi-Fi for sensitive logins — logging into your bank or university portal at a café without a VPN exposes your credentials to anyone on the same network.
* Don't mistake engagement for accuracy — a post with 50,000 likes and shares is not automatically true. Viral content spreads fast precisely because it triggers emotion, not because it's verified.
* Don't ignore cyberbullying or online harassment — whether it's directed at you or someone else, document it, report it through the platform, and don't normalize it as "just how the internet is."
