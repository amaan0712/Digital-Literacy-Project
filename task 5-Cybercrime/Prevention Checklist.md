\#  Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

\### For College Students in India



\---



\##  General Online Safety Tips



1\. \*\*Use strong, unique passwords\*\* for every account. Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across platforms (email, social media, banking).



2\. \*\*Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)\*\* on all important accounts — Gmail, Instagram, banking apps, and any platform storing personal data.



3\. \*\*Never click on suspicious links\*\* received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email — even if they appear to come from known contacts or official-looking sources (phishing attacks).



4\. \*\*Keep your software and apps updated.\*\* Security patches in updates protect against known vulnerabilities. Enable auto-updates on your phone and laptop.



5\. \*\*Avoid using public/college Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks\*\* like banking or logging into important accounts. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.



6\. \*\*Check app permissions regularly.\*\* Deny unnecessary access to camera, microphone, contacts, or location for apps that don't require them.



7\. \*\*Do not share personal information\*\* — Aadhaar number, date of birth, home address, or college ID — on social media or with unknown websites/apps.



8\. \*\*Log out of shared or public devices\*\* after use. Never save passwords in browsers on college lab computers or borrowed devices.



\---



\##  Financial \& UPI Safety Tips



* &#x20;\*\*Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank account details\*\* with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, NPCI, or government. Legitimate institutions never ask for these.





* &#x20;\*\*Beware of "UPI collect requests."\*\* Scammers send money-collect requests disguised as refunds or prize claims. Entering your PIN to "receive" money actually \*sends\* it. Always verify the purpose before approving any collect request.



\---



\##  How to Report Cybercrime



If you are a victim of any online fraud, cyberbullying, identity theft, or financial scam:



\-  \*\*National Cyber Crime Portal:\*\* \[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)

\-  \*\*Helpline Number:\*\* \*\*1930\*\* (National Cyber Crime Helpline — available 24/7)



Report immediately. Quick reporting increases the chance of recovering lost funds and helps authorities track offenders.
